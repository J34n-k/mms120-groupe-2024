How Online Dating Apps (Bumble, Tinder) Impact Traditional In-Person Dating

Understanding Disruptive Technologies

Technology has evolved to the point where digital products and services now offer new ways to enhance and improve work across various industries. These innovations are called "Disruptive Technologies" and the term was introduced by Clayton Christensen and Joseph Bower (Schuelke-Leech, 2017).

Such digital products are altering new methods of how consumers, industries, or businesses operate. These innovations sweep away established workflows and processes (Smith, 2022), which is why they’re considered disruptive.

  • Voice Assistants (Alexa, Siri, Google Assistant)

    These technologies transformed how individuals interact with digital systems. Voice assistants create new touchpoints for customer interactions, streamlining processes and reshaping industries like retail, marketing, and customer service.

  • Electric Vehicles

    EVs or Electric Vehicles pose challenges to traditional combustion engine vehicles, especially with the growing concerns over climate change and CO2 emissions.

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI)

    AIs are known to automate jobs, which help consumers skip time-consuming processes giving us more efficient results in complex tasks, most especially in education and healthcare industries.

  • E-Commerce Apps (Shopee, Lazada)

    These applications are shifting shopping from physical stores to online platforms, reducing the need for in-store visits.

  • Smartwatches (Apple/Samsung Watch)

    Smartwatches revolutionized how we associate with technology and manage personal health. By integrating health tracking, such as heart rate and sleep patterns, they challenge traditional fitness tools. They also offer new convenience by allowing users to receive notifications, make payments, and control apps directly from their wrist.

TRADITIONAL IN-PERSON DATING

Dating in person in the Philippines involves lots of nuances which stems from the old traditional dating cultures, particularly the custom of ligaw or courtship. Unlike in Western cultures, where the direct question “Will you be my girlfriend?” is common, in the Philippines, the process begins with the more formal and respectful “Can I court you?” This question signifies the suitor’s intention to prove himself worthy of the woman’s affection while respecting her autonomy in the decision.

Key Elements of Traditional Courtship

Ligaw (Courtship) Pagdalaw (Regular Visits) Harana (Serenade)

This is a period where the suitor demonstrates his sincerity and commitment through consistent effort, patience, and gestures of respect. For many, ligaw remains a cherished aspect of Filipino relationships today.

Consistently visiting the woman’s home allowed the suitor to build a connection with her and her family, reflecting the importance of familial approval in relationships.

A traditional way of professing love, a suitor would sing romantic songs outside the woman’s house, often accompanied by live music. This act symbolized both romantic interest and dedication.

CHALLENGES OF TRADITIONAL DATING

  • Societal Pressure and expectations

    Traditional dating involves a lot of factors, including pressures, expectations, and influences from family and the community. There are pressures to conform to societal expectations, such as dating for marriage, gender roles, and certain rituals and norms.

  • Limited Opportunities

    People are restricted when it comes to traditional dating, as it relies on personal connections, chance encounters, and social groups such as school, the workplace, or neighborhood, which limits options and exposure to diverse people and backgrounds.

  • Time-consuming

    Going on in-person dates, setting up dates, and attending various social events with the hopes of meeting someone new can take up a lot of time. Relationships also develop more slowly in traditional dating because of cultural norms, such as gradual courtship.

  • Financially Consuming

    Traditional in-person dating can be financially consuming, especially when going on multiple dates with the hopes of finding the right person. Dinner at a restaurant, transportation, entertainment, and gifts can become overwhelming. In traditional dating, there are unequal financial expectations due to gender roles and norms that expect that one partner, traditionally the man, to pay for most or all expenses.

  • Limited Access to Information

    Compared to modern dating, which uses dating apps that include profiles and backgrounds, traditional dating is a blank slate in which the initial impressions may be based on physical appearance or the interactions themselves when they meet face-to-face.

EMERGENCE OF ONLINE DATING APPS

Introduction to Online Dating Apps

Online dating apps once began as a digital way for users to express and share their interests with the intention of finding a significant other. The oldest known digital method for dating, long before the spread of the Internet, was created by two Stanford alumnus, Jim Harvey and Phil Fialer, called the Happy Families Planning Service. They gave questionnaires to 49 students, and put them through Stanford’s IBM 650 computer, running an algorithm that would pair their participants with each other using their questionnaire answers. While the resulting matches did not create long-term relationships, this was just the beginning for digital dating.

Moving past many other digital dating methods, sharing email addresses and joining online dating forums were all you needed to look for love online. While slower and inefficient compared to the modern version of online dating, these grassroot methods gave a more honest and in-depth experience between soon-to-be partners. As the Internet grew, online dating websites also took form, many of which would help people find their significant other overseas or across states.

However with the launch of Tinder in 2012, the online dating industry found its new tool to get users and business running with their swiping mechanism. Jonathan Badeen introduced the swiping mechanism to college students, their initial test demographic, incentivizing users to use the app by “gamifying” the dating process. Bumble followed the same formula, hoping to cater to a more feminist audience, and Grindr, an LGBTQ+ dating app predating Tinder, also applied the swiping mechanism.

DISRUPTION OF TRADITIONAL DATING

Dating apps have altered traditional methods of meeting love partners by reducing the reliance on social media for matching. Traditionally, individuals might meet possible partners through common friends, family gatherings, or social gatherings. However, sites such as Tinder and Bumble allow users to interact with a far larger pool of potential companions outside of their social groups. According to Smith (2023), this shift allows people to pursue relationships with people they may never meet in their daily lives, leading to a more diversified dating pool. Despite these benefits, the effectiveness of online dating has been challenged for encouraging shallow relationships. Many users indicate that relationships built through apps are less deep and stable than those formed through traditional channels (Johnson, 2022).

IMPACT ON INDIVIDUAL BEHAVIOR

Individuals' dating behaviors have also changed as a result of the influence of online dating platforms. According to Lee (2024), the anonymity and distance provided by these platforms might lead to "online disinhibition," in which users feel more free to express themselves in ways they might not in face-to-face conversations. While this might help with initial relationships, it can also lead to misrepresentation and unreasonable expectations, as individuals frequently design their online identities to attract more matches. Further, research shows that the growing use of dating apps can have a negative impact on mental health, causing anxiety and low self-esteem when interactions do not fulfill expectations (Davis, 2023). The quick pace at which relationships form online frequently accelerates physical intimacy while postponing emotional connection, changing how people approach romantic relationships.

DEMOCRATIZATION OF DATING

Dating apps have democratized the dating experience by making it more available to a wider range of people. According to Thompson (2022), they cater to a wide range of demographics, allowing users of varied origins, ages, and orientations to meet mates who share their interests. This accessibility lowers the barriers that traditionally excluded groups may experience in making romantic connections. In addition, online platforms frequently include features that allow users to filter possible matches based on specific criteria such as interests, values, and relationship goals (Roberts & Kim, 2023). This level of personalization contrasts strongly with traditional dating strategies, which rely mainly on accidental encounters or societal approbation. As a result, many people now consider internet dating a legitimate and productive way to find mates, leading to a cultural shift that normalizes looking for love online.

THE EFFICIENCY AND COMMERCIALIZATION OF ONLINE DATING

Online dating has allowed individuals to efficiently reach out to their potential partners due to technological advancements. However, it has gained a negative connotation for promoting superficial relationships and unreliable connections. According to Sharabi & Dorrance-Hall (2024), the tendency known as the ‘online dating effect’ results from online daters reporting fewer stable and fulfilling marriages compared to those who engage in traditional dating. Moreover, online dating apps profit from their users, focusing more on adding features that prompt users to curate their identities and pay for exclusive access to additional features, thereby increasing their exposure to more potential partners (French, 2022).

THE IMPACT OF ONLINE DATING ON RELATIONSHIPS AND SOCIAL NORMS

In addition to disrupting conventional methods of finding love, the rise of online dating coincides with a shift in attitudes and practices surrounding relationships and marriage, with more people putting off marriage or opting to stay single (Anderson et al., 2022). Furthermore, online dating has altered the pace at which individuals get to know each other, as connections formed online typically develop into sexual partnerships much more quickly than in traditional relationships (Ferguson, 2022). On the contrary, online dating offers individuals a more private space to interact with potential partners, often away from the social sphere, which contrasts with the public nature of traditional dating practices.

THE TRADE-OFF BETWEEN ACCESSIBILITY AND AUTHENTICITY IN ONLINE DATING

Likewise, online dating opens up a wider range of potential relationships, as users can set their preferences and location on these apps, allowing them to connect with more people who align with their interests. However, it can be harder to truly get to know others through a screen, as what is seen online often represents a curated version of themselves (Dunlop, 2018). In contrast, traditional dating typically involves forming relationships through acquaintances and face-to-face interactions, providing individuals with the opportunity to assess each other personally and gauge their intentions more clearly, in contrast to the digital realm of online dating.

Conclusion

The innovation of online dating apps like Tinder and Bumble shows a significant disruption compared to traditional dating practices, reshaping individuals’ romantic connections. With the disruptive technology, we can see that applications like these are much more convenient for accessibility and efficiency for finding your potential love. But having its advantages has its challenges as well, such as promoting superficial connections, privacy concerns, and safety risks.

In the Philippine traditional dating, it is part of our culture-rooted practice the “ligaw stage” and “harana” —courtship. The courtship stage should help two individuals to create a deep connection, but while these customs persist, a shift towards digital platforms will change the dating culture.

Online dating apps have made it easier to find your potential platform around the world. These platforms are also redefining the romantic expectations, prioritizing the digital personas in-person interaction.

As innovation evolves, the future can be defined more by AI-driven matchmaking. In conclusion, we cannot deny that dating apps offer better opportunities to communicate; they can also make relationships less deep compared to traditional dating.


But it is important that we understand how these changes could affect relationships and find ways to create and maintain your genuine connection in the digital world.
